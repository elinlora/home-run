Hi, there,

As you probably know, this newsletter is all about work — it even says it up there! But what about the Art of Hobbies? When you work full-time, it's important to unwind when you can — be it with a book, a movie, or a video game. But have you ever explored what else you could do? Sure, you might have taken a pottery class before, but have you considered geocaching, mushroom hunting or gingerbread house making? There's a world of hobbies out there, and it would be a waste if you wouldn't try a couple of weird ones. Before you know it, it might just turn into your next job.

Diversity

Goldman Sachs stops IPOs with all-male boards

— NY Post

The times they are a-changin'. Goldman Sachs has pledged to not take companies public if they have an all-male board of directors. While this might sound like a small change — the rules could have been stricter — it's actually a pretty big deal. Because the bank has been one of the world's most important underwriters of IPOs over the last decades, chances are that others will follow its lead.

— BBC

One moment you're working on something, but the next you find yourself mindlessly watching YouTube videos — it's only human. As we're living in the age of endless online content, it's far too easy to be distracted. According to traditional thinking, procrastination is part of having a time management problem. However, psychologists have recently been supporting a new theory — it has to do with the way we feel.
